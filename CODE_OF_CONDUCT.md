# Code of Conduct

JustCode exists to help developers learn, debug, and collaborate in a safe, friendly, and supportive environment.

By participating in this project, you agree to uphold these values and help create a positive community for everyone.

---

## Our Values

We believe that:
- Learning to code should feel approachable, not intimidating
- Questions are welcome, no matter your experience level
- Constructive feedback helps everyone grow
- Collaboration works best when people are respectful and kind

---

## Expected Behavior

All community members are expected to:
- Be respectful, patient, and considerate in discussions
- Encourage learning and knowledge sharing
- Provide constructive and helpful feedback
- Respect different viewpoints, backgrounds, and experience levels
- Take responsibility for their words and actions
- Credit sources appropriately when sharing code or ideas

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:
- Harassment, bullying, or repeated unwanted behavior
- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning language
- Discrimination or stereotyping based on personal characteristics
- Sexualized or inappropriate conduct
- Sharing private or sensitive information without consent
- Threats, encouragement of harm, or violence
- Spamming, trolling, or deliberately disrupting discussions
- Plagiarism or failure to credit original work

---

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:
- Open an issue on GitHub (if appropriate), or
- Contact the project maintainer through the repository

All reports will be handled respectfully and confidentially.
